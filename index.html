Introduction

One problem which can be observed by any city traveller or anyone who takes transport to and around Sydney is the circulation. There are various blocked and inefficient circulation points especially around Sydney Harbour. Individuals that wish to travel from one side (North Sydney) to the otherside (Balmain) have to take the Sydney Harbour Bridge which then they have to pay a toll price of $2.50 - $4. But what if we made travelling much faster without having to pay. That would be the more viable solution to inefficient circulation.

Our plan is to create a bridge system around the harbour, interconnecting the separate areas to create a "Bridge City". This city would be able to utilise unused space on water. Bridges will start from each "tip" of the city coasts and are then able to branch, link and overlap into hotels, outlets on water and/or an artificial beach. these attractions help with tourist and population growth.

Sydney Bridge City would have many motorways that get from point A to B without any obstruction of cities etc.. From these motorways will spiral into other main bridge roads and water hotels. There would be some peaks on the bridges, much like the peak on a wave, to act as a rest zone and lookout point. Most hotels would have a helipad because we believe the way to travel in the future is by air. Also, we do not want to be confined to the one Sydney Airport that is almost always overpopulated.

Thus Sydney Bridge City would be the greatest solution to dissolve traffic and circulation issues.
